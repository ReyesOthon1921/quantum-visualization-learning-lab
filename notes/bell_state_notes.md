\# Bell State Study Notes



\## What is a Bell State?



A Bell state is one of the simplest examples of quantum entanglement.



Two qubits become linked together so their measurements are correlated.



\## Circuit Components



\### Hadamard Gate (H)



Creates superposition.



Transforms:



|0> → (|0> + |1>) / sqrt(2)



\### CNOT Gate



Creates entanglement between qubits.



If control qubit is 1:

\- target qubit flips



\## Measurement Results



Expected outputs:



\- 00

\- 11



The Bell state should NOT commonly produce:

\- 01

\- 10



\## Why This Matters



Bell states demonstrate:



\- entanglement

\- quantum correlation

\- foundational quantum mechanics

\- quantum communication concepts
