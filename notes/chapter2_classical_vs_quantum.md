\# Classical Bits vs Qubits



\## Classical Bit



A classical bit exists in one state at a time:



0 or 1



Examples:



\- Light switch

\- Voltage high

\- Voltage low



\## Quantum Bit (Qubit)



A qubit can exist in a superposition:



|ψ⟩ = a|0⟩ + b|1⟩



The coefficients a and b are probability amplitudes.



Measurement collapses the state into either:



|0⟩ or |1⟩



\## Key Difference



Classical bits store one value.



Qubits store probability amplitudes until measurement.
